## Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

In recent years, UPI payment fraud has become one of the most common cybercrimes in India. This fraud typically involves scammers tricking users into authorizing fraudulent transactions.

A common scenario involves a victim receiving a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or customer support agent. The scammer claims there is an issue with the victim’s account or offers cashback rewards. They then send a “collect request” on UPI apps like Google Pay or PhonePe and instruct the victim to enter their UPI PIN to “receive money.”

However, entering the PIN actually authorizes a payment, transferring money to the scammer. In some cases, fraudsters also send fake links or QR codes, leading victims to phishing websites that steal sensitive details.

College students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, mental stress, and loss of trust in online systems.

For example, many victims have reported losing thousands of rupees within minutes due to such scams. This highlights the importance of awareness and careful handling of digital transactions.
